2. Oxidation

Why oxidize?

Si has dangling bonds SiO₂ creates bonds and passivates Si
We need doping masks and etch masks SiO₂ blocks dopant diffusion
The wafer might break SiO₂ is a shield

Growth Methods

Thermal Oxidation

  • The most common method
  • Involves heating silicon wafers in an oxygen-rich or steam environment

I. Dry Oxidation (O₂ Environment)

Si + O₂ → SiO₂
  • High-quality, dense SiO₂
  • Used for thin gate oxides
  • Slow growth

II. Wet Oxidation (H₂O / Steam Environment)

Si + 2H₂O → SiO₂ + 2H₂
  • Steam introduced via H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
  • Less density and more defects
  • Thicker oxides
  • 3 to 5 times faster

Other Methods

Name Principle Advantage
High-Pressure Oxidation Raising Pressure Faster growth at lower temperatures
Rapid Thermal Oxidation Rapid Thermal Processing Ultrathin and High-Quality
Plasma-Enhanced Oxidation Plasma at Low Temperatures Low Thermal Budget

Growth Kinetics: The Deal-Grove Model

tₒₓ² + A tₒₓ = B (t + τ)

  • tₒₓ = oxide thickness
  • A, B = rate constants
  • τ = initial native oxide correction
